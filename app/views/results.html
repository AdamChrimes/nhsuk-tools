Don't

  • do not try to do everything at once – set small targets that you can easily achieve
  • do not focus on the things you can’t change – focus your time and energy into helping yourself feel better
  • try not to feel like you're alone – most people feel low or anxious sometimes and support is available
  • try not to use alcohol, cigarettes, gambling or drugs to relieve a low mood - they can all contribute to poor mental health

Where to get NHS help for depression or anxiety

Referring yourself for therapy

If you need more support, you can refer yourself directly to a psychological therapies service without a referral from your GP.

This will help you to access free psychological therapies like cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) on the NHS.

{{ actionLink({ "text": "Find a psychological therapies service in your area", "href": "https://beta.nhs.uk/find-a-psychological-therapies-service/" }) }}

Non-urgent advice: See a GP if:

  • you’ve had a low mood for more than 2 weeks
  • you’re struggling to cope with anxiety or a low mood
  • things you're trying yourself are not working
  • you would prefer to get a referral from your GP

Urgent advice: Ask for an urgent GP appointment or call 111 if:

  • you need help urgently, but it's not an emergency

111 can tell you the right place to get help if you need to see someone. Go to 111.nhs.uk or call 111.

Immediate action required: Call 999 or go to A&E if:

  • you or someone you know needs immediate help
  • you have seriously harmed yourself – for example, by taking a drug overdose

A mental health emergency should be taken as seriously as a medical emergency.
